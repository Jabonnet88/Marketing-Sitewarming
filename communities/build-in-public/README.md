# Community — Build in Public

## What this community is
Builders, indie hackers, and developers who share their progress openly on Twitter/X. They post about products they're building, technical decisions, shipping updates, and lessons learned. High authenticity, high engagement rates, and a strong culture of supporting each other.

## Why SiteWarming engages here
Builders need domains. Whether they're launching an MVP, rebranding, or trying to establish credibility, their first real-world asset is often a domain. Engaging here builds recognition among people who are actively making those decisions.

## Key accounts to watch
- @slow_developer — thoughtful takes on AI, developer tools, and the future of building
- @karpathy — deep AI/ML insights, high reach
- @yongfook — indie hacker, vibe coding, product-market fit
- @VitalikButerin — builder mindset and systems thinking
- @toddsaunders — AI mindset and the gap between idea and product
- @PenuelAkom — technical specs, AI-assisted development
- @SofiaGonvela — early-stage building, learning in public

## Types of posts to engage with
- Shipping updates ("just launched X")
- Lessons from building ("what I learned after 30 days...")
- Hot takes on AI coding tools (Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot)
- Frustrations with the build process — great for CONTRARIAN comments
- Build-in-public milestones

## Engagement strategy
- Lead with acknowledgment, then go deeper than the obvious take
- DATA and CONTRARIAN comments perform best here — builders appreciate pushback
- Avoid generic encouragement — be specific about what you found interesting
- Never pitch SiteWarming directly — let the quality of the comment do the work
